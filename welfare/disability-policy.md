# Disability Policy and Benefits

*A forward-looking proposal, judged by what works, not by left or right.*

> **Summary for the reader**
> - **The problem:** the disability benefits system is stressful, often inaccurate, and costly to
>   run, while too few disabled people who want to work get the support to do so.
> - **The options:** support people into work with fair assessment, protect and uprate with light
>   assessment, or tighten eligibility to cut the bill.
> - **Lead option:** **support into work with accurate, humane assessment**, real employment
>   help for those who can work, and firm protection for those who cannot.
> - **Timeline:** reform assessment early; build employment support over a parliament.
>
> *Figures here are first-draft and will be checked in the budget-accuracy pass (#21).*

---

## The problem

Disability policy should help people live full lives and, where they can and want to, work. Too
often it does neither well.

**Assessments are stressful and inaccurate.** The process is adversarial and error-prone, with
many decisions overturned on appeal, which wastes money and grinds people down.

**Employment support is thin.** Many disabled people who want to work are not getting the
support that would let them, so talent and income are lost on both sides.

**Reform debates start from the wrong place.** They too often begin with cutting the bill rather
than with outcomes, which produces fear without saving much.

**The do-nothing baseline.** Leave this and the system keeps failing claimants and taxpayers at
once: stressful, inaccurate, and expensive, with disabled people locked out of work they could do.

---

## Evidence base

- **Good employment support works.** Where disabled people get tailored, well-funded help into
  suitable work, more move into employment and stay there. *Transferable* as the right focus.
- **Accurate assessment saves money.** Getting decisions right first time costs less than a system
  of appeals and overturns. *Transferable* as basic competence.
- **Security supports work.** People are more able to try work when they are not terrified of
  losing support if it does not work out. *Transferable* as the basis for fair conditionality.

---

## Options appraisal

**Option 1: Support into work with humane, accurate assessment *(lead option)*.** Fix assessment
to be accurate and less stressful, fund real employment support, and protect those who cannot
work. *Pros:* better outcomes, fewer appeals, more people in work, dignity preserved. *Cons:*
needs investment in support and a serious assessment overhaul. **Recommended.**

**Option 2: Protect and uprate, light assessment.** Prioritise benefit adequacy with minimal
reassessment. *Pros:* security and less stress. *Cons:* does less to open up work for those who
want it.

**Option 3: Control costs, tighten eligibility.** Focus on cutting the bill. *Pros:* short-term
savings. *Cons:* causes fear and hardship, pushes costs elsewhere, and saves less than hoped.

---

## The proposals (under the lead option)

1. **Fix assessment.** Make it accurate, evidence-led, and far less stressful, so decisions are
   right first time and appeals fall. Use medical evidence properly rather than tick-box
   processes.
2. **Fund real employment support.** Provide tailored help into suitable work for those who can
   and want to work, with employers supported to make reasonable adjustments.
3. **Protect those who cannot work.** Give firm, secure support to people who cannot work, without
   repeated pointless reassessment of conditions that will not improve.
4. **Make trying work safe.** Ensure people can attempt work without fear of losing support if it
   does not work out, so the system encourages rather than punishes effort.
5. **Improve accessibility.** Remove the barriers in transport, housing, and services that keep
   disabled people out of work and public life, linking to those chapters.

## Costs and benefits

Accurate assessment and good employment support cost money up front but reduce appeals, move
people into work, and cut the human and fiscal cost of a failing system. This connects to the
wider [welfare reform](./welfare-and-wellbeing-reform.md). Figures belong in the budget-accuracy
pass (#21).

---

## Delivery and timeline

- **Years 1-2:** overhaul assessment; protect those who cannot work from needless reassessment.
- **Years 2-5:** scale employment support and accessibility; embed safe routes to try work.
- **Years 5-10:** an accurate, humane system with more disabled people in suitable work.

---

## Risks and mitigations

- **Reform read as cuts by stealth.** *Mitigation:* be clear the aim is accuracy and support, not
  a smaller bill, and protect those who cannot work.
- **Employment support that does not deliver.** *Mitigation:* fund what is shown to work and
  measure outcomes.
- **Employers not making adjustments.** *Mitigation:* support and require reasonable adjustments.
