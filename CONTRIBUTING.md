# Contributing to Foundations for the Future

This is a living Green Paper, and the whole point is that you help shape it. I want the people
of this country to be able to contribute ideas directly, not just vote for a party once every
few years and hope for the best.

You do not need to be a policy expert. If you have lived experience, a good idea, a correction,
or a source that makes a proposal stronger, I want to hear it.

## How to contribute

1. **Open an issue.** Pick the template that fits:
   - **Share an idea** for something new or an improvement.
   - **Feedback on a proposal** to support, challenge, or refine an existing chapter.
   - **Challenge a figure or add a source** to keep the evidence honest.

   You can also open a blank issue if none of those fit.
2. **Open a pull request** if you want to suggest specific wording or add material.
3. **Start a discussion** about a theme or a trade-off.

Every chapter ends with consultation questions where the answer is not yet settled. Those are
good places to start.

## How feedback is handled

I read and assess contributions myself. Good ideas get folded into the documents, with the
reasoning shown. Issues that capture a real topic are kept open and labelled `documented` so
others can keep adding to them; only straightforward fixes get closed. Disagreement is welcome.
This is about working out what actually works, not scoring points, and I would rather publish my
reasons for not taking something forward than quietly ignore it.

## Ground rules

- **British English**, plain and direct.
- Be respectful. Argue the idea, not the person.
- Back claims with a source where you can. Real figures beat strong adjectives.
- All views are welcome, whichever "side" they would normally sit on. This project does not
  think in left and right.

## On privacy

You only need a free GitHub account to take part. There is no digital ID, and you do not have to
hand over personal details to contribute an idea.

Thank you for helping build this.
