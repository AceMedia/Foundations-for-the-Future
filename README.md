# Foundations for the Future

**A Green Paper for rebuilding Britain: practical reform, one foundation at a time.**

---

## About this document

*Foundations for the Future* is a **Green Paper**: a structured set of proposals and
**options for discussion**, not a finished manifesto. It is written in the style of
government policy documentation: each chapter sets out the problem, the evidence, the
options and their trade-offs, the costs, a delivery plan, and the risks, so that a
minister, official, or engaged citizen can read it and understand exactly what is being
proposed and why.

It is a **living document**. The proposals are meant to be tested, challenged, costed
more precisely, and improved through consultation.

---

## Forward, not left or right

Britain's problems will not be solved by sorting every idea into "left" or "right". That
binary is a relic, a way of grouping people and policies that no longer describes how the
country actually works, or what it needs. People are not two tribes, and good government is
not a tug-of-war between them.

This framework is **forward-thinking**. It starts from the problems in front of us:
unaffordable housing, a welfare system that manages crisis instead of preventing it,
regions starved of investment, dangerous Channel crossings, an NHS under strain, and asks
a single question of every proposal: **does it work, and does it build something better for
the future?** Where the best answer comes from Vienna's housing, Portugal's drug policy,
Germany's apprenticeships, or Estonia's digital state, we take it. The label it would wear
in Westminster is irrelevant.

---

## The big picture

Britain's crises are **interconnected**: poor housing drives NHS demand; a punitive
welfare system traps people out of work; weak skills hold back the regions; downstream
failure costs (temporary accommodation, reoffending, emergency care) quietly consume the
budget. Treating each in isolation is why reform keeps failing.

The core pattern of this Green Paper is to **secure essentials first** (housing, food,
warmth, care, safety), **cut avoidable crisis spending**, **build productive capacity**
(skills, procurement, regional enterprise, resilience), and **fund repair visibly**. See
[FRAMEWORK-MAP.md](FRAMEWORK-MAP.md) for how the chapters reinforce one another.

## What we're proposing

Each link below opens a full chapter. Figures quoted here are **indicative headlines**;
the detailed costings and sources live in the [budget analysis](budget/summary-of-savings.md).

### 🏠 Housing: guaranteed homes, not landlord subsidies
Redirect the billions spent on Housing Benefit that flows to private landlords into
council housing Britain actually owns. Secure homes, lower homelessness costs, warmer
buildings, fewer cladding scandals.
→ *[Housing policy](housing/housing-policy.md)*

### 💷 Welfare: investment in people, not crisis management
Replace cash-and-hope with direct provision: secure housing, bulk food procurement,
covered essentials, and well-being budgets for dignity and growth. Finland and others show
prevention costs less than crisis.
→ *[Welfare and well-being reform](welfare/welfare-and-wellbeing-reform.md)* ·
*[Bulk food procurement](welfare/bulk-food-procurement.md)*

### 🚢 Immigration: legal routes, fast decisions, smart enforcement
Expand safe legal routes, decide asylum claims in months not years, target smuggling
networks, and remove the pull of illegal work, while rebuilding the capacity to help
upstream instead of paying downstream. Treat seasonal migration as a genuine two-way
exchange, sending British workers abroad as readily as we receive workers here. Lessons
from Australia, Canada, and Germany.
→ *[Immigration framework](immigration/immigration.md)* ·
*[Seasonal worker programmes](immigration/seasonal-migration.md)*

### 🛡️ Defence: capability, aid, and resilience
Rebuild Britain's defence, logistics, engineering, and aid capacity so the state can deter
threats, support allies, respond to emergencies, and intervene upstream rather than paying
ever-larger downstream failure costs.
→ *[Defence and resilience framework](defence/defence-and-aid-resilience.md)*

### ⚖️ Justice: rehabilitation that pays back
Give low-risk offenders structured public-service work, qualifications, and a real route
into employment on release, in food production, care, retrofit, logistics, and community
repair, with strict safeguards and incentives tied to compliance.
→ *[Rehabilitation through public service](justice/rehabilitation-through-public-service.md)*

### 🏭 Economy: entrepreneurs everywhere, not just London
Spread prosperity beyond the capital with regional venture funds, infrastructure
investment, incentives for risk-taking, and green enterprise.
→ *[Economic renewal](economy/entrepreneurship-and-renewal.md)*

### 🩺 Health: prevention, not just treatment
Treat drugs as a health issue, expand bulk healthy-food provision, and focus NHS resources
on care rather than policing lifestyle. Where adults use regulated vices, tax them by harm
and recycle the revenue into treatment, recovery, social care, and end-of-life dignity.
→ *[NHS and public health](health/nhs-and-public-health.md)* ·
*[Vice taxation and betterment](health/vice-taxation-and-betterment.md)* ·
*[End-of-life and dementia care](health/end-of-life-and-dementia-care.md)*

### 🎓 Education: skills for real jobs
Match education to the jobs that exist (coding, engineering, green technology) on the
German apprenticeship model, with strong second-chance routes back into learning and work.
→ *[Education and skills](education/education-and-skills.md)*

### 🚆 Transport: reliable journeys, a connected North
Fix the local-roads backlog, bring buses back under local control on the Manchester model,
and build east-west rail so the North works as one economy rather than separate towns.
→ *[Transport](transport/transport.md)*

### 🗳️ Governance and the constitution: local power, fair votes, a listening state
Move power out of Westminster, reform the Lords and the voting system, build a second base
for government in the North, let people shape policy between elections, and run a leaner
digital state without a national digital ID.
→ *[Constitution and devolution](governance/constitution-and-devolution.md)* ·
*[Public participation](governance/public-participation.md)* ·
*[Local government](governance/local-government.md)* ·
*[Governance and the digital state](governance/democracy-and-reform.md)*

## The numbers (indicative)

These reforms are designed to **save more than they cost** by preventing failure rather
than managing it. The headline ranges below are order-of-magnitude estimates; each is
broken down and sourced in the budget chapter, and refining them is part of the
consultation.

| Area | Indicative annual saving | Source of saving |
|---|---|---|
| Housing | £1–2bn | Redirecting Housing Benefit; ending temporary accommodation |
| Health | £0.5–1bn | Warmer homes and better nutrition cutting NHS demand |
| Welfare | £0.2–0.5bn | Less bureaucratic churn and crisis intervention |
| Skills | £0.8–1.5bn | Fewer NEETs; higher productivity |
| Government | £0.6–1.2bn | Merged agencies and digital transformation |

**Indicative total: ~£3–6bn a year in direct savings**, before dynamic growth effects or
any earmarked revenue from regulated vice taxation.
→ *[Full budget analysis](budget/summary-of-savings.md)*

## Why this matters

Britain has done bold, structural reform before: the post-war settlement, council
housing, the founding of the NHS. It can do so again. What it takes is honesty about
trade-offs and a willingness to learn from what demonstrably works elsewhere: Vienna's
social housing, Canada's refugee sponsorship, Germany's skills training, Portugal's drug
policy, Estonia's digital government. This Green Paper sets out how those lessons could
work here.

## Taking part

This is a living document, and the point is that you help shape it. I want people to be able to
contribute ideas directly, not just vote for a party once every few years. You do not need to be
an expert, and all views are welcome whichever side they would normally sit on.

- **Open an issue** using one of the templates: share an idea, give feedback on a proposal, or
  challenge a figure and add a source.
- **Submit a pull request** with revisions or new material.
- **Start a discussion** around a theme or trade-off.

Every chapter ends with **consultation questions** where the answer is not yet settled. I read
and assess contributions myself, and issues that capture a real topic stay open for others to
build on. See [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md) for how it works. You only need a free GitHub
account to take part, and there is no digital ID. The thinking behind all this is set out in the
[public participation chapter](governance/public-participation.md).
