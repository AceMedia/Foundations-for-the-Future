# Phasing and Delivery Plan

*The delivery annex for this Green Paper: how the proposals are sequenced, judged by what works.*

This file turns the framework into a practical sequence. It is the delivery annex rather than a policy
chapter, so it sets out phasing, pilots, and dependencies instead of options for consultation. The
goal is to avoid treating every idea as a day-one national rollout. Some reforms should start as
pilots, some need legislation, some need budget appendices first, and some can be delivered through
existing systems.

---

## Policy Choices Already Set

These choices should guide the next edits across the repo:

- **Prison rehabilitation:** broad access can apply to low-level non-violent offending, with cautious case-by-case access for anything above non-violent theft or similar low-harm acquisitive offending. Serious violence, sexual offending, domestic abuse, coercive control, exploitation, terrorism, and high safeguarding risk should be excluded from public-facing and care-facing routes.
- **Sentence incentives:** incentives should be proportional to both the offence and the work. Higher-effort, higher-value tasks can earn stronger incentives, but only within public-safety limits.
- **Care funding:** protect ordinary savings while improving care quality. Use health-and-care-focused vice-tax receipts, bulk procurement, avoided hospital costs, and supervised public-service rehabilitation to reduce pressure on families.
- **Vice-tax allocation:** vice-tax receipts should go to health and care first: treatment, mental health, social care, end-of-life dignity, medical relief, recovery, and community health.
- **Food procurement:** food support should be segmented by age, illness, and household need rather than delivered as one generic package.

## First 100 Days

### 1. Create delivery boards

Set up four cross-department delivery boards:

| Board | Lead departments | First task |
|---|---|---|
| Health and Care Betterment Fund | DHSC, Treasury, DWP, local government | Set vice-tax earmark scenarios and care priorities |
| End-of-Life Support Passport | DHSC, NHS England, DWP, councils | Design one trigger route for benefits, energy, care, equipment, and palliative support |
| Public Service Rehabilitation Corps | MoJ, HMPPS, DWP, DfE, councils | Select pilot prisons/probation areas and define eligibility |
| Bulk Food and Nutrition Procurement | DEFRA, DHSC, DfE, DWP, councils | Define segmented food packages and pilot logistics |

### 2. Freeze the fiscal principles

Publish three accounting rules:

- direct savings, avoided costs, and earmarked revenue must be shown separately;
- uncertain effects stay **TBD** until modelled;
- no saving can be counted twice across housing, health, welfare, justice, or food procurement.

### 3. Choose pilot areas

Pick 10-15 mixed pilot areas covering:

- urban, rural, coastal, and post-industrial communities;
- areas with high care pressure;
- areas with food insecurity;
- resettlement prisons or open prisons;
- councils with capacity to test digital coordination.

## Year 1 Pilots

### End-of-life and dementia care

Pilot:

- End-of-Life Support Passport;
- energy social tariff trigger;
- rapid adaptations and equipment delivery;
- tailored food and carer meal support;
- hospice-at-home and palliative advice line expansion;
- Personal Dignity Allowance options.

Measure:

- time from trigger to support;
- emergency admissions;
- delayed discharge;
- family/carer stress;
- place of death where preferences are known;
- cost per supported person.

### Food procurement

Pilot segmented packages:

- children and families;
- working-age welfare households;
- older people;
- end-of-life and frailty;
- medical diets;
- recovery and mental health support.

Measure:

- food cost per household;
- nutritional quality;
- waste levels;
- delivery reliability;
- health and social-care feedback;
- user dignity and choice.

### Justice rehabilitation

Pilot:

- food growing, packing, and logistics;
- repair, recycling, and public estate maintenance;
- non-personal care-support training;
- Community Payback with qualifications;
- ROTL paid work where risk assessed.

Measure:

- attendance and completion;
- qualifications;
- breaches, absconds, and safeguarding incidents;
- employment at 6 weeks and 6 months;
- reoffending at 12 and 24 months;
- levy receipts and release savings.

## Years 2-3 Scale-Up

Scale only what passes tests.

| Reform | Scale condition |
|---|---|
| End-of-Life Support Passport | Faster support and fewer repeated assessments |
| Hospice-at-home | Evidence of reduced avoidable admissions or better preferred-place outcomes |
| Food segmentation | Lower cost or better nutrition than retail/emergency provision |
| Justice work pathways | Safe operation, better completion, employment gains, no worker displacement |
| Vice-tax earmark | Published receipts, transparent allocation, public reporting |

Legislation likely needed:

- statutory basis for vice-tax earmarking;
- data-sharing powers for End-of-Life Support Passport;
- social care charging/cap reform;
- stronger funeral price transparency if not already sufficient;
- sentence incentive rules for earned compliance and work/training.

---

## Years 4-5 National Rollout

National rollout should include:

- Health and Care Betterment Fund annual statement;
- national End-of-Life Support Passport;
- national dementia supplement and care-cap model;
- bulk food procurement in every region;
- approved public-service rehabilitation placements;
- local dashboards showing outcomes and spending.

By this stage, budget files should be updated from **TBD** to scenario ranges where evidence supports it.

## Dependencies

| Policy | Depends on |
|---|---|
| Care cap and dementia supplement | Treasury/DHSC modelling and legislation |
| End-of-Life Support Passport | NHS, DWP, councils, and energy supplier data coordination |
| Bulk food procurement | suppliers, kitchens, logistics, nutrition standards |
| Public-service rehabilitation | risk assessment, supervision, employer partnerships, legal incentive rules |
| Vice-tax funding | duty forecasts, ring-fence rules, public reporting |
| Housing adaptations | councils, housing providers, repair workforce, equipment supply |

## Next Build After This

The next best substantive addition after the social-care costing refinement is a tighter **justice savings refinement**. The rehabilitation chapter is now one of the clearest cross-cutting delivery ideas in the repo, but it still needs harder numbers on:

- target cohorts;
- custody days avoided;
- wage-levy and victim-support flows;
- training and supervision costs;
- reoffending and employment effects;
- what can be treated as cashable savings versus wider social benefit.
