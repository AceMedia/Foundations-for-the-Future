# Framework Map and Next Build Priorities

This repo is strongest when each policy area reinforces the others. The core pattern is:

- **secure essentials first**: housing, food, warmth, care, safety;
- **reduce crisis spending**: prevent avoidable NHS, prison, homelessness, and asylum costs;
- **build productive capacity**: skills, procurement, defence logistics, regional enterprise;
- **fund repair visibly**: betterment funds, vice-tax receipts, and published savings;
- **protect dignity**: welfare, end-of-life care, rehabilitation, and local community life.

This file maps the current documents, shows the strongest cross-links, and suggests the next best additions.

## Strongest System Loops

### Housing -> Health -> Budget

Warm, safe, stable homes reduce respiratory illness, falls, mental stress, delayed discharge, and homelessness pressure. This supports:

- [housing/housing-policy.md](./housing/housing-policy.md)
- [health/end-of-life-and-dementia-care.md](./health/end-of-life-and-dementia-care.md)
- [budget/housing-savings.md](./budget/housing-savings.md)
- [budget/health-savings.md](./budget/health-savings.md)

Next improvement: add more explicit housing links to rapid adaptations, dementia-safe homes, and end-of-life discharge prevention.

### Food Procurement -> Welfare -> Health -> Justice

Bulk food procurement lowers costs and improves nutrition. It can also provide work routes through supervised rehabilitation pathways.

- [welfare/bulk-food-procurement.md](./welfare/bulk-food-procurement.md)
- [welfare/welfare-and-wellbeing-reform.md](./welfare/welfare-and-wellbeing-reform.md)
- [health/end-of-life-and-dementia-care.md](./health/end-of-life-and-dementia-care.md)
- [justice/rehabilitation-through-public-service.md](./justice/rehabilitation-through-public-service.md)

Next improvement: create a food procurement operating model with suppliers, kitchens, delivery routes, workforce, and pilot areas.

### Vice Taxation -> Health and Care Betterment

Regulated vice revenue is treated as earmarked repair money rather than hidden Treasury intake.

- [health/vice-taxation-and-betterment.md](./health/vice-taxation-and-betterment.md)

### Justice -> Skills -> Public Services -> Lower Reoffending

Low-risk offenders can repair public value, gain qualifications, and reduce future custody pressure.

- [justice/rehabilitation-through-public-service.md](./justice/rehabilitation-through-public-service.md)

### Northern Powerhouse -> Governance -> Economy -> Transport

A strong, fast-growing Greater Manchester can spread prosperity across the North if national government disperses out of London and the northern cities are properly connected.

- [economy/entrepreneurship-and-renewal.md](./economy/entrepreneurship-and-renewal.md) (Manchester as the proven regional anchor)
- [governance/constitution-and-devolution.md](./governance/constitution-and-devolution.md) (a permanent second base for Parliament and government in the North)
- a future transport chapter (east-west links so the powerhouse reaches the whole North, not just Manchester)
- local government and devolution (the Greater Manchester devolution model as the template)

Next improvement: write the transport chapter, settle how far the second-base idea should go (see the governance options), and develop a regional-rebalancing thread tying economy, governance, transport, and local government together.

### Immigration -> Defence/Aid -> Seasonal Work -> Food Security

Migration policy works better when it combines faster decisions, legal routes, upstream crisis response, and practical labour routes.

- [immigration/immigration.md](./immigration/immigration.md)
- [immigration/seasonal-migration.md](./immigration/seasonal-migration.md)

## Recommended Next Additions

### 1. Refine the Justice Savings Appendix

**Update:** `budget/justice-savings.md`

The social-care appendix is now materially stronger. The next best addition is to tighten the justice numbers so the rehabilitation chapter carries the same fiscal weight.

It should include:

- cost per prison place;
- target cohorts for low-risk work pathways;
- wage levy receipts;
- victim-support contributions;
- custody days avoided;
- reoffending reductions;
- employment after release;
- costs of training, supervision, transport, and safeguarding.

Why first: the justice proposal is practical and politically useful, but it still needs tighter numbers to avoid overclaiming.

### 2. Refine the Delivery and Phasing Plan

**Update:** `implementation/phasing-and-delivery.md`

It should show what happens in:

- first 100 days;
- year 1 pilots;
- years 2-3 scale-up;
- years 4-5 national rollout;
- which department owns each reform;
- what legislation is needed;
- which reforms are pilots, which are national, which are budget-only.

Why second: the repo is becoming a system, and delivery detail is now the main execution gap.

### 3. Refine the Workforce Costings and Outcomes

**Update:** `budget/education-and-skills-savings.md`

It should quantify:

- vacancy and churn effects in care and logistics;
- conversion rates from short certificates to apprenticeships;
- guaranteed-interview effects on hiring;
- prison-leaver employment outcomes;
- agency-spend reductions and avoided delivery bottlenecks.

Why third: the workforce architecture is now clear; the next step is to quantify how much pressure it can actually remove.

### 4. Refine the Betterment Revenue Appendix Over Time

**Update:** `budget/health-and-care-betterment-revenue.md`

It should keep pace with:

- updated OBR receipt forecasts and outturns;
- any changes to alcohol, tobacco, or gambling tax policy;
- any new legal-market consultation on cannabis or other adult-use products;
- local distribution formulas and reporting requirements.

Why fourth: the structure now exists; it should be maintained as policy changes, not treated as finished forever.

### 5. Deepen the Social Care Appendix Further Over Time

**Update:** `budget/social-care-and-end-of-life-savings.md`

It should later add:

- nation-by-nation cohort splits;
- stronger cashability tests for hospital offsets;
- better links between homecare expansion and discharge pressure;
- fuller treatment of surviving spouses and modest-asset households.

Why fifth: the file is now usable, but still worth revisiting as better data appears.

## Cross-Reference Maintenance Rules

When adding a new policy chapter:

- link it from [README.md](./README.md);
- add its fiscal impact or TBD modelling need to [budget/summary-of-savings.md](./budget/summary-of-savings.md);
- add at least one link from a related topic chapter;
- add evidence sources directly in the chapter;
- add a corresponding budget file if the proposal includes headline savings or costs.

When adding a new budget chapter:

- link back to every narrative chapter that supplies the mechanism;
- separate direct savings from avoided costs and earmarked revenue;
- mark uncertain figures as **TBD** until modelled;
- avoid counting the same saving twice across files.
